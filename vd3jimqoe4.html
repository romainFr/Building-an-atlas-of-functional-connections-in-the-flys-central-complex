Figure \ref{704309} outlines some of the observed connectivity patterns. Three types of inputs were isolated (Figure \ref{704309}C): ring neurons provide inhibitory inputs to the wedge columnar neurons (Figure \ref{704309}Ai), as was suggested previously \cite{Hanesch1989,Kahsai_2012,Mart_n_Pe_a_2014}. One of the ring neurons we examined, the gall ring neuron, likely participates in a feedback loop, because it receives excitatory input from P-EG neurons (Figure ). One class of LAL-NO interneurons provides another class of inhibitory input into the EB columnar system by targeting the P-EN neurons (Figure \ref{704309}Aii). It is likely that other types of LAL-NO interneurons innervating other noduli compartment target P-FN neurons, but these pairs have not been tested extensively yet. Finally, several columnar neurons share excitatory inputs in the PB, from a PB-IB-SPS neuron (Figure \ref{704309}Aiii). In contrast, we found few potential output channels. The only such pathway identified in this dataset is presented in figure \ref{704309}C and connects the PF3-LALCre columnar neuron to a LAL interneuron. It is important to note here that FB tangential neurons, which we did not include in our experiments, may contribute potential output pathways as well.
