Finally, connectivity in the PB is much sparser than predicted solely from anatomy (figure \ref{704309}A). The main observation is that the E-PG, the only columnar type presynaptic in the PB, seems to not being connected directly to any of the other columnar neurons in the PB. This connection is indirect, through the  \(\Delta7\) interneurons, which therefore seem to constitute an important bottleneck in the system.

Discussion

The presented dataset will constitute a useful ressource for the growing community of researchers interested in the central complex. Whereas similar coarse functional connectivity techniques have been used in individual studies, this is to our knowledge the first extensive dataset of this kind. It will hopefully become an evolving source of information, which will take its full value when combined with other data source : in our opinion, coarse functional connectivity associated with EM-level anatomical connectivity and high resolution gene expression profiles would constitute a very solid base to shape constrained models of the structure and form detailed hypothesis. As any large dataset, it is mainly a starting point for more detailed research. 
It is worth elaborating on the limitations of the method to insure a correct interpretation of the data presented. First, the combination of CsChrimson and GCaMP does not strictly insure that the responses observed are direct. However, the large set of non overlapping controls provides a statistical framework to interpret the results in that respect -- uncertainty is only present for weak connections. A more pressing issue concerns the sensitivity.  What can be detected is bounded by the stimulation protocol and the sensitivity of GCaMP6-m. This means that an absence of response cannot be formally be interpreted as an absence of connection. The fact that inhibitory responses are visible, and that clear responses saturate with the range of stimulations used (see supplementary figure XX) is reassuring, but it is likely that EM-level anatomy will reveal that a fraction of the weak synaptic connections have been missed, and will need to be investigated using more sensitive, likely electrophysiological, methods. In general, comparing results from different methods will point at specific points of interest requiring more detailed studies. Finally, the full-field stimulation of full neuronal type populations protocol used here has a couple of drawbacks. We have no access to connectivity between neurons of the same class, and potential unphysiological network effects could arise. Fly stocks and crosses

For every LexA driver used, we prepared two stocks containing GCaMP6-m \cite{chen_ultrasensitive_2013} and CsChrimson \cite{klapoetke_independent_2014} under LexAop (resp. UAS) or UAS (resp. LexAop) control : XXX-LexA;13XLexAop2-IVS-p10-GCaMP6m 50.629 in VK00005, 20xUAS-CsChrimson-mCherry-trafficked in su(Hw)attP1 and XXX-LexA;20xUAS-IVS-GCaMP6m 15.629 in attP2, 13XLexAop2-CsChrimson-tdTomato in VK00005. Those stocks were then crossed to a Gal4 driver or a split-Gal4 \cite{luan_refined_2006} driver for the experiment. In the split-Gal4 case, the two split halves are inserted in attP40 and attP2 respectively. To avoid transvection between the split and the LexA driver \cite{mellert_transvection_2012}, we inserted the LexA drivers in alternative sites, either su(Hw)attP5 \cite{pfeiffer_refinement_2010} or VK22 \cite{venken_pacman:_2006}, and used the splits exclusively in combination with those lines.
