The range of observed responses is exposed in figure \ref{489066}A. Upon stimulation, effects ranged from a total absence of change (figure \ref{489066}Avi) to very large reliable transients (figure \ref{489066}Ai). In between those extremes, transients of variable size and reliability could be observed.
Interestingly, we could also observe inhibitory responses. This was rendered possible by the fact that -- at least in some cell types -- the baseline fluorescence was fluctuating during the experiment (Quantify the spread of baselines for each cell type and the population if it makes sense) : even if hyperpolarizations below the resting potential are likely not detectable through GCaMP imaging (), inhibition from an excited state do appear as dips in the fluorescence trace (figure \ref{489066}Aii). 
Intriguingly we noticed that even for excitatory responses, the amplitude of the transient seemed to be dependent on the baseline level at the time of stimulation (figure SXX).
