Effects of stimulation ranged from very large and reliable transients (Figure \ref{489066}Ai) to a total absence of change (Figure \ref{489066}Aiii). In between those extremes, we observed transients of variable size and reliability (Figure \ref{489066}B). Interestingly, we also detected inhibitory responses (Figure \ref{489066}Aii). This was possible because — at least in some cell types — baseline fluorescence fluctuated and was occasionally elevated during the experiment (see Discussion and Supplementary Figure \ref{998826}). Therefore, even if hyperpolarization below the resting potential is likely not detectable through GCaMP6m imaging, inhibition from an excited state appears as a dip in the fluorescence trace.
