In addition to 41 potential pairs, the screen also included 23 pairs of neighboring but non-overlapping neurons to control for the specificity of the responses observed, and 6 direct-activation controls in which CsChrimson and GCaMP6m were expressed in the same neurons.

Characterization of responses

The range of observed responses is summarized in figure \ref{489066}A. Effects of stimulation ranged from very large and reliable transients (Figure \ref{489066}Ai) to a total absence of change (Figure \ref{489066}Aiii). In between those extremes, we observed transients of variable size and reliability (Figure \ref{489066}B). Interestingly, we also detected inhibitory responses (Figure \ref{489066}Aii). This was possible because — at least in some cell types — baseline fluorescence fluctuated and was occasionally elevated during the experiment (see Discussion and Supplementary Figure \ref{998826}). Therefore, even if hyperpolarization below the resting potential is likely not detectable through GCaMP6m imaging, inhibition from an excited state appears as a dip in the fluorescence trace .
