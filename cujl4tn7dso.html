Since no single measurement could recapitulate the complexity of the observed responses, we decided to summarize the transients obtained with a battery of statistics reflecting the amplitude, shape, reliability and stimulus sensitivity of the response (see figure \ref{489066}B and methods for a full description). Responses of non-overlapping pairs were then used to form the null-hypothesis distributions of the statistics (figure \ref{489066}C and SXX). Pairs were considered as connected if significantly outside of this multivariate distribution (see Methods for details), and response strength was measured in terms of the Mahalanobis distance to the null distribution.
